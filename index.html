Opening

Further along on paper than almost any peer.

Northeastern is Anthropic's first university design partner. Claude for Education is live for 50,000 students, faculty, and staff across 13 campuses. The Institute for Experiential AI runs 90-plus affiliated faculty, an Applied AI Solutions Hub, and a Responsible AI Practice. On paper the AI posture is further along than almost any peer institution. The interesting question is not whether Northeastern should adopt AI. It is how adoption translates into measurable shifts in teaching, research, and co-op placement at the college and campus level.

The Eduba frame

60 / 30 / 10.

We work with a simple decomposition: in most institutions about 60 percent of the workload is traditional software and database work, about 30 percent is rule-based logic, and about 10 percent is genuinely AI-shaped. Teams that skip that distinction end up putting an LLM where a database would be faster, cheaper, and more auditable. Applied to a federated university, the question becomes which parts of curriculum delivery, co-op matching, advising, and research support actually want an agent, which want a workflow, and which want a person with a playbook. That is the orchestration question, and it is the question the Anthropic partnership eventually has to answer at the process level.

The relevant case study

Academic register, academic precedent.

Our closest reference point is an academic collaboration with the University of Edinburgh's UKICER network, where the methodology work was framed as research-grade rather than vendor engagement. The posture transfers: work at the Khoury College or Institute for Experiential AI level would look like co-authored methodology, not a procurement line item. At Flagler College we ran a small-cohort faculty engagement focused on pedagogy under AI, which is the closer precedent for anything that lands inside Miami Innovation Academy or Align cohorts.

The relevant paper

Interpretable Context Methodology.

Our methodology paper, "Interpretable Context Methodology: Folder Structure as Agent Architecture," was submitted to ACM TiiS and is public at github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-. It argues that agent context can be organized as a layered filesystem with measurable interpretability gains. The framing maps naturally onto how a 13-campus university thinks about context, governance, and reproducibility.
